# Workspace Architecture: Open Biosciences

> Analysis date: 2026-03-01
> Scope: 13 repositories, 9 agents, 4 architectural layers
> Generated by: Claude Code (direct analysis, not RA orchestrator)

## Key Findings

- **13 repos** organized into 4 layers (Foundation, Platform, Orchestration, Validation) with clear ownership across a 9-agent team
- **biosciences-mcp** is the platform's center of gravity: 12 FastMCP servers, 697+ tests, consumed by all 3 orchestration-layer repos
- **Fuzzy-to-Fact protocol** (ADR-001 S3) is the unifying pattern — enforced across all MCP servers, agent systems, and skills
- **Wave 4 (Validation)** is in progress — evaluation, education, and workspace-template repos are planned but not yet populated
- **marketplace** packages all platform assets (12 servers, 6 skills, 9 SpecKit commands) into 15 Anthropic `.claude-plugin/` format plugins

## Platform at a Glance

Open Biosciences is a multi-repo AI-powered biosciences research platform. It wraps 12 life sciences APIs (HGNC, UniProt, ChEMBL, Open Targets, STRING, BioGRID, Ensembl, Entrez, PubChem, IUPHAR, WikiPathways, ClinicalTrials.gov) in a unified FastMCP gateway, then layers LangGraph multi-agent supervision, PydanticAI durable workflows, and RAG evaluation pipelines on top. Results persist to a Graphiti/Neo4j knowledge graph.
